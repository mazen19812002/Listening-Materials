Speaker 1: In our communication skills class, we were asked to talk about how engineers make requests, invitations, and suggestions. I think this is very useful for teamwork. Speaker 2: Absolutely. Clear and polite communication is essential for engineers when working in teams or with clients. Speaker 1: Let’s start with requests. When making a request, we need to be clear and concise. State exactly what we need and why we need it. Speaker 2: And it’s important to be respectful. We shouldn’t sound demanding or rude. Tone makes a big difference in professional communication. Speaker 1: For example, if I need help with a coding task, I could say, “Could you please help me with this task? I’m having some trouble with the code.” Speaker 2: That’s a good example. Another one could be requesting resources: “I’m writing to request additional resources for our team. We’re working on a new project and we need more manpower and equipment.” Speaker 1: We can also request feedback politely. For example, “I’d like to get your feedback on my design. I’m still not sure if it’s the best approach.” Speaker 2: Exactly. Requests should always be clear, polite, and explain the reason for the request. Speaker 1: Now, what about invitations? Engineers often need to invite colleagues to meetings or project discussions. Speaker 2: Yes. An invitation should include the purpose, time, and place. For example: “I’d like to invite you to a project meeting on Monday at 10 a.m. to discuss the new design proposal.” Speaker 1: That’s clear and professional. It helps the person understand why their attendance is important. Speaker 2: We should also be polite when inviting clients or external partners. A phrase like “We would be pleased if you could attend…” sounds more formal and respectful. Speaker 1: Good point. Now let’s talk about suggestions. Suggestions are important when proposing improvements or new ideas. Speaker 2: Yes, and they should be constructive. Instead of criticizing directly, we can say, “Perhaps we could consider another approach,” or “How about trying a different testing method?” Speaker 1: That sounds collaborative rather than critical. Speaker 2: Exactly. Suggestions should encourage discussion, not create conflict. Speaker 1: So overall, whether we are making requests, invitations, or suggestions, clarity, politeness, and professionalism are essential. Speaker 2: I agree. Effective communication strengthens teamwork, builds trust, and improves project outcomes.
